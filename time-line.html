A History Website of Johannes Gutenberg and printing

The invention of movable-type


In the earliest record, the first movable-type was invented by a Chinese inventor called Bi Shing at around 1040AD using clays.

Gutenberg's Journey


Around mid 15 century, Gutenberg designed a punch-matrix system which allows printers to make casting mould in a printing machine.

Printing the Gutenberg's Bible


Around 1450s, a Mainz banker Johann Fust was interested in investing on Gutenberg's printing machine. So He was able to hire workers such as Peter Schoeffer for printing the Gutenberg's bible

Other publications


Another book that could relate to Gutenberg's technology was Catholicon in 1460, a Latin dictionary written by Joannes Balbus.

Development


Soon after Johann Fust's death in 1466, his son-in-law Peter Schoeffer led the business of Fust & Schoeffer until 1502.

spreading across Europe


The technology of printing was later spread through other European countries such as France, Italy, England and Spain in the 15th Century.
